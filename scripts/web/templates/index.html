Archive Settings

Tesla records dashcam footage to the USB drive in a circular buffer called RecentClips. After about an hour, older clips are automatically overwritten by new ones. The archive service copies these clips to the Pi's SD card before they're lost, so you can browse and cloud-sync them later.

When enabled, clips are automatically copied from the USB drive to the SD card every 2 minutes. Disable this if you don't need local copies of your dashcam footage.

When your car is parked or idle, Tesla still records footage — but these clips have no GPS data and are usually just a static view of a parking lot. Enabling this skips those idle clips and only archives footage from times when you were actually driving (based on indexed trip data). This can save 50–80% of SD card space. If no trips have been indexed yet, all clips are archived as a safety fallback.

Archived clips older than this are automatically deleted to free up SD card space.

Archival stops when SD card free space drops below this. Protects the OS and other services from running out of disk.

Mapping & Indexing

Tesla embeds GPS coordinates and telemetry data (speed, braking, steering) inside each dashcam video. The indexer extracts this data and builds a database of trips, routes, and driving events. This powers the map view — showing where you drove, plotting routes, and detecting events like hard braking or sharp turns.

Scans dashcam videos to extract GPS waypoints and build trip routes. When disabled, the map page won't show any trips or routes. Videos can still be browsed by folder.

When the Pi connects to WiFi (e.g., you arrive home), also scan archived clips on the SD card for GPS data. This ensures older trips that were archived from RecentClips still appear on the map. Disable if indexing takes too long or you only care about recent trips on the USB drive.
