The root filesystem is mounted; an online repair isn't possible. Scheduling a check creates /forcefsck, which makes systemd-fsck run e2fsck -fy on every fstab filesystem at the next boot, before they're mounted read-write. The check typically adds ~30 s to boot time on a healthy SD card. Reboot after scheduling for the check to actually run.

SD cards expose no wear telemetry (no SMART, no per-block checksums). Plan to replace the card every 12 months and keep cloud archive enabled so a card failure never costs you data.
